A Computational Fragment-Screening Approach for Designing New Type I Kinase Inhibitors

Joint work with Grigorii Andrianov, Ilya Serebriiskii, and John Karanicolas.

Short Description

Fragment based drug design is a technique that allows us to eschew large-scale docking of lead compounds. We design a new computational pipeline for designing potent and selective kinase inhibitors and test our pipeline against 5 libraries and 4 kinase targets (CDK9, CDK2, CHK1, and ACK1).

Poor Generalization by Current Deep Learning Models for Predicting Binding Affinities of Kinase Inhibitors

Joint work with Kirubakaran Palani and John Karanicolas.

Short Description

Current deep learning models provide surprisingly accurate predictions of kinase-inhibitor binding affinity. Our work investigates if this high performance is representative of advances in cheminformatic deep learning or symptomatic of larger issues in developing algorithms for such tasks.
