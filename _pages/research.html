A Computational Fragment-Screening Approach for Designing New Type I Kinase Inhibitors

Joint work with Grigorii Andrianov, Ilya Serebriiskii, and John Karanicolas.

Short Description

Fragment based drug design is a technique that allows us to eschew large-scale docking of lead compounds. We design a computational pipeline leveraging the principles of fragment-based drug design to develop novel and selective kinase inhibitors.

Poor Generalization by Current Deep Learning Models for Predicting Binding Affinities of Kinase Inhibitors

Joint work with Kirubakaran Palani and John Karanicolas.

Short Description

Current deep learning models provide surprisingly accurate predictions of kinase-inhibitor binding affinity. Our work investigates if this high performance is representative of advances in cheminformatic deep learning or symptomatic of larger issues in developing algorithms for such tasks.

Deep Learning: New Insights from Module Theory

Short Description

Deep learning has traditionally been understood through the lens of linear algebra as a series of operations on vectors, matrices, and tensors even though we understand these algorithms to be non linear. Modules over algebraic rings are generalizations of vectors over fields. Will a view of deep learning based on functors and natural transformations be more comprehensive?
